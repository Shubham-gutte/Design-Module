Visual hierarchy

Visual Hierarchy

The visual specification can be easily understood by the user. The following are some important aspects of representing:

Alignment:

An element with a different alignment attracts more attention.

Consistency:

Each element's consistency in margin and padding is flawless.

WhiteSpace:

More white space between elements draws more attention to them.

Colors:

Bright colors are more noticeable than muted ones.

Example:

As an example, two mobile screens On the right-side image, there are two sections of the menu and stories On the right mobile screen, there are no specific alignments of items, and no consistency is maintained. There is no specific white space around the items. On the other hand, there is the consistency of aligned items, and perfect white space is obtained.

Color Scheme

Color Scheme

A UI color scheme is a mixture of colors used in your user interface. Almost every color scheme contains the following groups of colors:

Primary color :

The most frequently used colour in our design.

Secondary color :

It also provides more ways to accent and distinguishes your product.

Accent colors :

Used to emphasize some UI elements such as buttons or progress bars, or to highlight some information.

Neutral colors :

Act as a supporting character. Usually, these colors are used for the background and text.

Semantic colors :

To denote standard value states (such as good, bad, or warning),

Iconography

What is iconography??

Iconography is a visual language used to demonstrate aspects of content or functionality, Icons are meant to be simple and clear, visual elements that are recognizable immediately.

Keep in mind that icons need to be understood easily by users, so make sure to show consistency in your design.

Size of Icons:

16px(small size of icons)

24px(medium size of icons)

32px(large size of icons)

Here are some important points that you need to take care of.

Test Scalability :

Make sure your Icons are visible at small size.

Internally Consistance

Use the same color scheme for all icons and also the same styling attribute.

Perfect Alignment

Always try to have perfect alignment in iconography.
